NFHS4Vis is an outcome of a joint effort at IIIT Bangalore between the Graphics-Visualization-Computing Lab (GVCL) and the E-Health Research Center (EHRC). This project has been funded by the Shared University Grant by IBM India. This project is into building visual analytics tools and techniques for understanding the raw data of population surveys, for which we have chosen National Family Health Survey, specifically NFHS-4. NFHS-4 data describes the health indicators of all of India in a snapshot of time, in 2015-16.

The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoH&FW) of the Government of India (GoI) and the World Health Organization (WHO) Country Office for India (WCO) have jointly published the Country Cooperation Strategy (CCS) in 2017. The CCS states the important challenges in the public health sector, which include “the unfinished agenda of health system modernization” and “the need for expediting progress in achieving Millennium Development Goals (MDG) 4 and 5”. MDG-4 and 5 pertain to child health, undernutrition, and gender equity.
